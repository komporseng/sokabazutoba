Chanse Corbi Job

Chanse Corbi is a medical student who has done a few stints as a babysitter. She revealed on her LinkedIn profile that she served as a medical assistant intern at St. Joseph Health from August 2020 to June 2021.

She has always aimed to establish a career that included the spotlight. Her first introduction to fame was competing in pageantry. Chanse was the contestant and winner of Calabasas Teen 2018 and was an official preliminary to Miss California Teen. Rather than flaunting her beauty, she took the competition as the platform to discuss teen issues, bring awareness to cancer research, and be a positive influence in Calabasas and its surrounding.

After being crowned, Chanse attended various community events including Susan G Komen, an MS walk, and literacy events at the Children’s Foundation.

Since elementary school, athletics was a huge part of her life. Chanse was a varsity cheerleader at Oaks Christian School where she also competed in lacrosse and track. She competed in 100 Meter Dash (placed 9th) and Pole Vault (placed 1st).

At the time, she had shared about pursuing a career as a neurosurgeon and oncology research.

Her pageantry gig also opened up doors for her to work as a model. She has landed a paid partnership with Endorsement. co.

Chanse has also received one credit as an actress in The Girl on the Roof in 2018.

Talking about her education, Chanse is attending Chapman University where she is studying Bachelor of Business Administration. She also studied Health/Health Care Administration/Management and expected to graduate in June 2023. She is also studying biochemistry hoping to earn her Bachelor of Applied Science in 2023.

Chanse writes on her LinkedIn bio, “I have always been fascinated by the medical industry on the patient care and administrative ends. I have been studying business and health sciences at Chapman University to improve my knowledge of the field. I hope to be able to use my skills to help others in order to be a part of something bigger than myself, especially during this time of crisis.”
