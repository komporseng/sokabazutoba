The Mesopelagic Fish Floor: Wall Of Fish In The Ocean Theory Meaning

Because it is in the midst of the ocean, between the epipelagic zone, which receives the most light, and the bathypelagic zone, which receives no light, the mesopelagic zone is also known as the twilight zone.

Fish, shrimp, squid, snipe eels, jellyfish, and zooplankton are among the creatures that may be found in this zone.

According to recent studies, the biomass of fish in the twilight zone might be 10 times more than previously thought in the rest of the ocean combined.

Many animals living in the twilight zone make their own light through a process known as bioluminescence to attract predators and attract food in order to survive in such low light.

Some species in the twilight zone dwell in its shadows, while others migrate to and from the ocean’s surface every day in the world’s greatest animal migration.

This movement perplexed US Navy sonar operators during WWII since sonar scans appeared to show the sea floor altering depth – hence, the wall of fish.
