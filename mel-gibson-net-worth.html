Mel Gibson Career Highlights

Mel Gibson was born in Peekskill, New York, on January 3, 1956. When he was 12 years old, he relocated to Australia with his family. In 1977, he made his film debut in Summer City after studying acting at the National Institute of Dramatic Art.

He became an international star after starring in the Mad Max trilogy (1979–1985). He also acted in the Lethal Weapon franchise, which was one of the highest-grossing action-comedy series of all time (1987–1998).

Gibson has demonstrated his abilities as a filmmaker and producer. Braveheart (1995), a historical epic about Scottish warrior William Wallace, earned him two Academy Awards for Best Director and Best Picture.

He also directed and produced The Passion of the Christ (2004), a contentious but immensely successful film portraying Jesus Christ’s final hours. The picture grossed nearly $600 million worldwide, making Gibson one of Hollywood’s wealthiest filmmakers.

Mel Gibson Personal Life

Mel Gibson married Robyn Moore in 1980, and the couple produced seven children: six sons and one daughter. After 31 years of marriage, they divorced in 2011. Gibson’s divorce was one of the most expensive in Hollywood history, costing him half his fortune.

He has two further children: a daughter with Russian singer Oksana Grigorieva and a son with American writer Rosalind Ross. Throughout his career, Gibson has been involved in a number of issues and scandals. Various sources have accused him of anti-Semitism, homophobia, racism, misogyny, and domestic abuse.

He has also struggled with drinking and melancholy and has been arrested multiple times for DUI. He has expressed regret for some of his actions and words, and he has sought treatment for his problems.
