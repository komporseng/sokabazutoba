Her Disease & Condition Progeria Syndrome Update

Progeria is a rare, progressive genetic disorder that causes children to age rapidly in their first two years of life.

At birth, children with progeria appear normal. Signs and symptoms such as slow growth and hair loss begin to appear during the first year.

Most children with progeria die as a result of heart problems or strokes. A child with progeria has a life expectancy of about 13 years. Some people with the disease die young, while others live for a long time, even up to 20 years.
